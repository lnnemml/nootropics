# 0003 — No Soviet/Russian Origin Story as Primary Marketing Angle

- **Date:** prior session (carried over from memory), recorded in wiki
  2026-06-28
- **Status:** Confirmed — hard constraint

## Decision

The Cold War / Soviet cosmonaut / 1996 Olympic doping-scandal origin story
for bromantane must **not** be used as the leading hook, headline, or
primary narrative angle in any customer-facing marketing material
(landing pages, ads, emails, social posts).

## Context

This is an explicit instruction from Anton, carried over from prior
project sessions. The underlying scientific/historical facts (Soviet-era
actoprotector research, dopamine mechanism studies, the 1996 doping
context) remain valid as supporting/credibility material and can still
inform internal reasoning about *why the compound works*, but the
"forgotten Soviet secret" framing itself is excluded from anything a
customer sees.

## Consequences

- The "Rediscovered Breakthrough" angle family
  (see [`../marketing/messaging-angles.md`](../marketing/messaging-angles.md))
  is constrained: usable for mechanism/credibility detail, not for
  headlines or the primary narrative arc of an advertorial.
- Any new advertorial draft, ad copy, or email sequence must be checked
  against this constraint before being finalized. If in doubt, ask
  "would removing the words Soviet/Russian/Cold War/cosmonaut break this
  piece of copy?" — if yes, it's relying on the excluded angle and needs
  rework.

## Revisit if

Anton explicitly reverses this instruction in a future session — record
the reversal here with a date and reasoning rather than silently dropping
the constraint.
