# 0002 — Primary Advertising Angle: "Restore, Don't Stimulate"

- **Date:** prior session (carried over from memory), recorded in wiki
  2026-06-28
- **Status:** Confirmed

## Decision

Static ad creative uses the **"Restore, Don't Stimulate"** angle as the
primary format, executed as a contrast split-panel design (stimulant
spike/crash visual vs. NeuroDrive's flat sustained line), structured as
Problem-Agitate-Solution or Before-After-Bridge.

## Context

Five candidate angle families were identified from research (see
[`../marketing/messaging-angles.md`](../marketing/messaging-angles.md)):
Rediscovered Breakthrough (Soviet secret), The Dopamine Reset, Focus
Without Stimulants, Mental Endurance Engine, and Curiosity/Story Hooks.
"Focus Without Stimulants" tests as the strongest direct-response angle
because it's the most universally-applicable pain point across the entire
avatar (caffeine/Adderall fatigue is near-universal in this audience) and
requires the least pre-education to land, unlike the Soviet-secret angle
which needs a longer setup and is constrained anyway (see ADR 0003).

## Consequences

- Static ad templates and the NeuroDrive landing page hero should default
  to this contrast framing.
- Other angles remain valid for specific channels (e.g. Mental Endurance
  Engine for LinkedIn/developer-targeted ads) but are secondary, not
  default.

## Revisit if

A/B testing in Phase 1+ shows another angle materially outperforming this
one on CTR or CVR.
