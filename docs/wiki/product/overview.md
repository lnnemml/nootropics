# NeuroDrive — Product Overview

> Source: `docs/raw/foundational/NeuroDrive_offer_brief.pdf`,
> `NeuroDrive_research_document.pdf`. Last synthesized: 2026-06-28.

## What it is

NeuroDrive is a sublingual bromantane solution (30ml dropper bottle,
pharmaceutical-grade MCT oil carrier) positioned as a **stimulant-free
cognitive performance restorer** — not another stimulant in a crowded
nootropics market.

## Big Idea

Unlock deep drive and clarity — without stimulants, crashes, or fog — using
a dopamine stabilizer originally developed for Soviet cosmonauts and
military pilots, now rediscovered for modern high-performers.

## Positioning anchor

**"Restore, don't stimulate."** This is the single sentence every page,
every ad, every email should be checkable against. If a piece of copy could
just as easily be said about caffeine or Adderall, it's off-brand.

## Unique Mechanism of the Problem (UMP)

Modern life overstimulates dopamine pathways (notifications, hustle
culture, stimulant cycling) and burns them out — producing fatigue, fog,
and motivation loss. This is *not* the user's personal failing; it's a
systemic depletion with a specific, nameable mechanism.

## Unique Mechanism of the Solution (UMS)

Bromantane restores dopamine tone through a non-stimulant, adaptogenic
mechanism (upregulating tyrosine hydroxylase / dopamine synthesis capacity
over time, rather than forcing acute dopamine release). It works *with*
the brain's own system, not against it. See
[`docs/wiki/product/mechanism-and-science.md`](./mechanism-and-science.md)
*(to be ingested — see backlog in `log.md`)* for the deeper scientific
narrative (Soviet "actoprotector" origin, 1996 Olympic doping story,
dopamine/anxiolytic dual action).

## Discovery story (founder narrative angle)

A "rogue neuroscientist / performance hacker" framing: researching
forgotten Soviet-era actoprotectors, discovering bromantane's track record
with military pilots and cosmonauts, personally testing it, being struck by
the absence of jitters or crash.

**Hard constraint:** Anton has explicitly excluded the Soviet/Russian
*origin-story-as-headline* angle from marketing materials. The science and
history can still inform internal reasoning and mechanism explanations, but
should not be the leading hook in customer-facing copy. See
[`docs/wiki/decisions/0003-no-soviet-origin-angle.md`](../decisions/0003-no-soviet-origin-angle.md).

## Format & delivery

Sublingual drops — faster absorption than capsules, easy dose titration.
This is a real differentiator vs. single-ingredient capsule competitors
and "kitchen sink" stacks (Alpha Brain, Mind Lab Pro, Thesis).

## Where this product sits in the broader platform

NeuroDrive is **product #1** of a multi-product nootropics e-commerce
platform (see [`docs/wiki/architecture/platform-vs-product.md`](../architecture/platform-vs-product.md)).
Every data model and page template should assume more products will follow,
even though only NeuroDrive ships in Phase 1.
