# NeuroDrive — Buyer Avatar

> Source: `docs/raw/foundational/NeuroDrive_avatar_sheet.pdf`,
> `NeuroDrive_research_document.pdf`. Last synthesized: 2026-06-28.

## Who they are

- Age 24–42, ~70% male, US / Canada / Western Europe / Australia
- Programmers, startup founders, product designers, quant researchers,
  engineers, biohackers, academic researchers
- Personal income $5k–$25k/month; many run their own startup or freelance
  business
- Self-identity: rational optimizers, performance hackers, systems
  thinkers, "builders, not consumers"

## What they're done with

- Caffeine/Adderall cycles that end in tolerance, jitters, or a crash
- "Kitchen sink" stacks (Alpha Brain, Mind Lab Pro) that are either inert
  or inconsistent
- Hype-driven supplement marketing with no real mechanism behind it

## What they actually want

A way to feel sharp **without trading it for a crash later** — calm,
sustained drive, not a wired spike. They want to trust that something
works, not just hope it does.

## Core identity belief

*"My brain is my leverage — I can't afford to let it slip."* Falling behind
cognitively isn't just inconvenient to this person, it threatens their
sense of identity (builder, top performer, "the productive one").

## Emotional journey (use this to structure page flow, not just copy)

1. **Awareness** — current systems/supplements aren't delivering
2. **Frustration** — tried mainstream/hyped options, inconsistent or
   side-effect-laden results
3. **Desperation & seeking** — Reddit, forums, YouTube research for
   something safe, legal, that actually works
4. **Relief & commitment** — discovers bromantane/NeuroDrive, feels the
   difference, integrates it as a daily ritual

This maps directly onto the sales-page flow already validated for the
NeuroDrive landing page: **Belief → Problem → Failed Solutions →
Mechanism → Transformation → Proof → Urgency → Action.**

> "I just want something that helps me feel sharp without needing another
> cup of coffee."
> "I'm not trying to get high — I'm trying to feel normal again, without
> the fog."
> "Everything works... until it doesn't."
> "I'm so sick of feeling like a zombie by 3pm."
> "It's like turning the lights back on in my brain."

## Implication for product/site design

- Tone throughout the site: rational, evidence-first, "for builders" — not
  wellness/spiritual, not bro-science hype.
- Never use language that implies the product "fixes" them — they resist
  being framed as broken. Use "support," "restore," "optimize."
- Identity call-outs ("for founders, coders, researchers who can't afford
  an off day") work well as section headers, not just ad headlines.
