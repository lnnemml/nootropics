## Why the placeholder copy failed

Claude Design wrote this without access to the wiki's product/avatar/
beliefs pages — only `brand-design-brief.md` was in its project, and the
Design pass #1 calibration explicitly told it to "write plausible
placeholder copy true to the brief's voice." That produces competent,
generic "calm-focus startup" copy — true to the *visual* brief, blind to
the *positioning* brief. It could describe Levels, Eight Sleep, or any
calm-tech brand. Nothing in it requires NeuroDrive specifically. Lesson
for next time: hand Claude Design the product/marketing wiki pages too,
not just the design brief — see backlog note in `log.md`.

## Hero

- **Eyebrow:** Built for people who can't afford an off day
- **H1:** Restore, don't stimulate. *(unchanged — validated positioning, ADR 0002)*
- **Subhead:** Three cups of coffee and you still hit a wall by 3pm. Adderall works until it doesn't. NeuroDrive is a non-stimulant dopamine stabilizer — bromantane, in pharmaceutical-grade sublingual drops — that restores your baseline instead of borrowing against it. No spike. No crash. Just the clarity you remember having.
- **CTA:** See the mechanism
- **Chart caption:** unchanged — `actoprotector class · not a stimulant`

## Contrast / "the two curves"

- **Eyebrow:** unchanged — The two curves
- **H2:** unchanged — One mechanism lifts your floor. The other rents you a ceiling. *(this line tested well — keep it)*
- **Card 1 — NeuroDrive / Restore:** A measured rise that holds, day after day. People don't describe it as a boost — they describe it as the fog finally lifting, and staying lifted.
- **Card 2 — Stimulants / Spike & Crash:** A borrowed peak, paid back with interest — the jitters, the crash, and a tolerance that creeps up until the same dose barely moves the needle.

## Mission

- **Eyebrow:** unchanged — Mission
- **Body:** NeuroDrive exists because modern dopamine pathways are running on debt. Notifications, stimulant cycling, and hustle culture overdraw the system that's supposed to give you drive — and caffeine or Adderall just postpones the bill. Bromantane was developed to help people perform under real strain without burning out the system that makes performance possible. We didn't invent that mechanism. We just refused to bury it behind another stimulant with better branding.

## Principles

1. **Mechanism over marketing** — unchanged: Every claim traces back to a pathway we can name and a study we can cite. No proprietary fairy dust.
2. **Built for skeptics** *(replaces "Evidence, not vibes")* — You've tried things that didn't work. Good — that's the right instinct. We'd rather you read the mechanism than take our word for it.
3. **No spike, no crash** — unchanged: Designed to lift your floor, not your ceiling. The goal is a longer steady state, not a bigger high.
