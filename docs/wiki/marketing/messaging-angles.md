# NeuroDrive — Messaging Angles & Headline Bank

> Source: `docs/raw/foundational/NeuroDrive_offer_brief.pdf`,
> `NeuroDrive_research_document.pdf`. Last synthesized: 2026-06-28.

## Primary angle (current default for static ads)

**"Restore, Don't Stimulate"** — contrast split-panel format (stimulant
spike/crash vs. NeuroDrive's flat, sustained line). Use Problem-Agitate-
Solution or Before-After-Bridge structure. See
[`../decisions/0002-primary-ad-angle.md`](../decisions/0002-primary-ad-angle.md).

## Five validated angle families

1. **Rediscovered Breakthrough** (Soviet secret) — curiosity/history hook.
   ⚠️ Use mechanism facts only, not as the headline — see hard constraint
   in [`../product/beliefs-and-objections.md`](../product/beliefs-and-objections.md).
2. **The Dopamine Reset** (Fall from Eden) — "it's not your fault, modern
   life drained your drive; this restores baseline."
3. **Focus Without Stimulants** (No Crash, No Jitters) — direct contrast
   to caffeine/Adderall. **This is the primary angle (#1 above) in
   practice.**
4. **Mental Endurance Engine** (actoprotector/performance identity) — "for
   builders, coders, founders who can't afford an off day."
5. **Curiosity/story hooks** for top-of-funnel ads (e.g. "I tried the
   ex-cosmonaut pill for a month" style first-person narrative format) —
   used carefully given the origin-story constraint; lead with personal
   experiment narrative, not nationality/history framing.

## Headline/subheadline options (validated set)

- "Feel Focused. Clear. Unshakably Driven." / "A dopamine stabilizer — not
  a stimulant. NeuroDrive restores your edge without the crash."
- "For Coders, Builders, and Thinkers Who Outperform the System." /
  "NeuroDrive flips your dopamine switch back on — so you can go deeper,
  longer."
- "Turn the Lights Back On in Your Brain." / "NeuroDrive clears the mental
  fog and restores that 'in the zone' feeling — with zero caffeine."

## Sales page structure (validated, do not deviate without reason)

**Belief → Problem → Failed Solutions → Mechanism → Transformation →
Proof → Urgency → Action.**

Each transition between sections should be an explicit argument bridge,
not a hard cut — see copywriting principle in `log.md` entry
`2026-0X-XX`.

## Credibility levers that outperform generic social proof

- "Don't Buy If..." qualifying sections
- Testimonials with measurable before/after metrics
- Logic-driven urgency (rational supply-constraint explanation, not fake
  countdown timers)
